The team module handles short-lifecycle squads for "playing one session together": create a team, invite members, start the game, dissolve. This is a different concept from the guild's "persistent organization."

## What problem does it solve?

In multiplayer games, players need to form a squad before entering matchmaking or an instanced dungeon. Building your own team system means handling:

- Concurrent invites causing overcapacity (two players invited simultaneously; after one joins, the other would push it over the limit)
- Who takes over when the leader leaves; when to auto-dissolve
- Team state management (recruiting / full / in-game) to prevent duplicate starts
- How to quickly route solo players into the same team during quick match

The team module encapsulates all of this logic — business code just needs to handle the "start game" action.

## Typical use cases

**Scenario 1: Multiplayer dungeon squad**
Player creates a 4-person team, shares an invite link with friends. Friends accept and join. When all 4 are ready, the leader taps "start game" — team status becomes in_game, the dungeon begins.

**Scenario 2: Quick match**
Players who don't want to manually form a team tap "quick match." The system automatically places them into an open team or creates a new one while waiting for others.

**Scenario 3: Leadership succession**
The leader goes offline unexpectedly. With `autoDissolveOnLeaderLeave=false`, the system automatically promotes the member who joined earliest to leader — the team continues.

**Scenario 4: Ranked mode team queue**
Before entering ranked matchmaking, players form a fixed team using the team module. The full team enters the matchmaking pool together, with shared match weight.

## Core capabilities

- **Temporary team management**: create, invite, accept/decline, kick, dissolve — full lifecycle API
- **State management**: open (recruiting) / closed (full) / in_game / dissolved
- **Concurrency safety**: version field prevents overcapacity under concurrent writes; conflicts returned to caller for retry
- **Leader policy**: on leader departure — configurable auto-dissolve or auto-succession to earliest member
- **Quick match**: optional mode that automatically routes solo players into suitable teams

## Who is it for?

| Role | What they get |
|------|--------------|
| Decision-makers | Multiplayer experience is a core differentiator; the team module lowers the implementation barrier |
| Product managers | Flexible configuration of team size, invite TTL, and matching strategies |

| Engineering team | Standard API — concurrency safety is module-guaranteed, no need to implement version locking yourself |
