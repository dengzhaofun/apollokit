The level module implements a structured "chapter → level → star rating" system: levels have unlock rules, completion earns stars, full stars yield bonus rewards, and all progress is persisted server-side — no dependency on client-side local saves.

## What problem does it solve?

Most games have linear content progression, but building the level system yourself runs into:

- Varied unlock conditions (pass the previous level / accumulate stars / complete a chapter) — each requiring special handling
- Is the star rating additive or max? (Player replays and gets 2 stars when they previously had 3 — should it drop?)
- Idempotency of reward claiming — the same star's reward must not be claimable twice

The level module standardizes these rules. Designers configure in the dashboard; the client only needs to report completion results.

## Typical use cases

**Scenario 1: Linear chapter progression**
10 chapters, 100 total levels. Chapter 2's first level is locked until Chapter 1 is fully completed. Players must unlock in order; the server automatically validates unlock eligibility.

**Scenario 2: Star-based bonus rewards**
Up to 3 stars per level. 1-star earns the base reward; 3-star earns additional rare items. Rewards are claimable independently — players can 1-star the level now and come back to grind 3 stars later.

**Scenario 3: Varied unlock conditions**
A secret level requires 200 total stars across the map; another requires clearing a specific prerequisite level. Multiple unlock rule types supported through configuration.

**Scenario 4: Event-exclusive levels**
Limited-time levels available only during an event. Automatically archived when the event ends, without affecting main-story level progress.

## Core capabilities

- **Chapter management**: multi-chapter structure with independent unlock conditions per chapter
- **Flexible unlock rules**: auto-open / pass specific level / accumulate stars / complete chapter — all configuration-driven
- **Star system**: up to N stars per level (configurable); stars take the historical maximum, never decrease on replay
- **Per-star rewards**: each star independently configured and independently claimable — idempotent
- **Server-side saves**: all progress persisted server-side and synced across devices — no local saves required

## Who is it for?

| Role | What they get |
|------|--------------|
| Decision-makers | Centralized linear content progress management — prevents save-loss complaints |
| Product managers | Flexible unlock conditions and reward structures for complex level designs |

| Engineering team | Just report completion results — unlock validation and reward delivery are handled by the module |
